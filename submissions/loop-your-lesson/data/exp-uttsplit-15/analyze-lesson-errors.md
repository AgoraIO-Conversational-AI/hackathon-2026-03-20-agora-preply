### Pattern 1: Dative case avoidance/confusion

The student systematically struggles with dative case forms, either using accusative where dative is needed (przerywac mnie -> mi), using dative where accusative is needed (przedrzezniac mi -> mnie), or using analytical 'dla + genitive' constructions to avoid dative altogether (zaproponowalem dla niego -> zaproponowalem jemu). This is the most significant recurring pattern.

**Recommended practice:** Targeted exercises on Polish verbs that require dative complement: pomagac komus, przerywac komus, proponowac komus, przeszkadzac komus, dziekowac komus. Contrast with accusative verbs: widziec kogos, przedrzezniac kogos, znac kogos.

### Pattern 2: Preposition transfer from Ukrainian

The student consistently transfers Ukrainian preposition patterns into Polish: 'za' instead of 'o' (klocic sie), 'od' instead of 'przed' (ukryc), 'na' instead of 'do' (wsiasc), 'od' instead of 'niz' (comparatives). This is the second most frequent error pattern and directly attributable to L1 transfer.

**Recommended practice:** Preposition-verb collocation drills focused on common Polish verb+preposition combinations that differ from Ukrainian: klocic sie O cos, ukryc PRZED kims, wsiadac DO samochodu, uciekac PRZED kims. Fill-in-the-blank and multiple-choice exercises.

### Pattern 3: Masculine personal plural forms

The student uses non-masculine-personal plural forms where masculine personal forms are required: 'wszystko/te' instead of 'wszyscy/ci', 'nauczyciele' (nominative) instead of 'nauczycieli' (accusative). The masculine personal category does not exist in Ukrainian.

**Recommended practice:** Exercises specifically targeting masculine personal plural: demonstrative pronouns (ci/tamci vs te/tamte), universal pronouns (wszyscy vs wszystkie), and accusative = genitive rule for masculine personal nouns.

### Pattern 4: Reflexive particle omission/misplacement

Occasional omission of obligatory reflexive 'sie' (denerwowac sie, podobac sie) and non-native placement of reflexive particles (wyobrazic sobie instead of sobie wyobrazic). While Ukrainian has similar reflexive constructions, the clitic placement rules differ.

**Recommended practice:** Review of common reflexive verbs with practice on natural 'sie/sobie' placement in various sentence structures.
