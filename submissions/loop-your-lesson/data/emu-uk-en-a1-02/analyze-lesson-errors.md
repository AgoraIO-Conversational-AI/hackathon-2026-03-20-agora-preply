# Error analysis: emu-uk-en-a1-02

> Applied the error taxonomy (grammar, vocabulary, pragmatic categories) with severity calibrated to A1 CEFR level. Used Ukrainian-English interlanguage patterns to identify L1 transfer errors - particularly article omission (Ukrainian has no articles), preposition confusion ("o" -> "on"), and third person -s omission (Ukrainian verbs do not inflect for person in the same way). Severity is calibrated against A1 expectations: article errors are moderate (expected at this level but must be addressed), while third person -s omission is major (it is the core grammar target of this lesson and recurs persistently).

## Errors

### #1 grammar/article_omission (moderate) [00:25]

**Original:** I am working on new project.
**Corrected:** I am working on a new project.

Missing indefinite article "a" before "new project". At A1, articles are being introduced and are expected to be inconsistent, but this is a high-frequency pattern that recurs throughout the lesson.

- **Communicative impact:** low
- **L1 transfer:** yes - Ukrainian has no article system. Nouns appear without determiners, so Ukrainian speakers systematically omit English articles.
- **Correction strategy:** recast - Sarah rephrased with "a new project" without flagging it explicitly
- **Exercise priority:** 4

### #2 grammar/missing_subject (moderate) [00:40]

**Original:** Is... application. Mobile application.
**Corrected:** It's an application. A mobile application.

Missing subject "it" and missing article. Ukrainian is a pro-drop language that allows subject omission, especially with copular constructions.

- **Communicative impact:** low
- **L1 transfer:** yes - Ukrainian allows null subjects (pro-drop). "Це додаток" does not require an explicit subject pronoun.
- **Correction strategy:** none - teacher did not address the missing subject here
- **Exercise priority:** 3

### #3 grammar/subject_verb_agreement (major) [00:58]

**Original:** client want many things
**Corrected:** the client wants many things

Missing third person -s on "want" and missing article "the". This is the single most recurring error pattern in the lesson.

- **Communicative impact:** medium
- **L1 transfer:** yes - Ukrainian verbs do not add a suffix for third person singular present tense in the same way. The conjugation system is different, so the English -s/-es ending has no direct equivalent.
- **Correction strategy:** recast - Sarah rephrased with "the client wants"
- **Exercise priority:** 5

### #4 grammar/modal_syntax (moderate) [01:32]

**Original:** I must to say time correct.
**Corrected:** I must say the time correctly.

Added "to" after "must". Also missing article before "time" and adverb form "correctly".

- **Communicative impact:** medium
- **L1 transfer:** yes - Ukrainian "мушу" (must) is followed by an infinitive form, and learners transfer the "to" from "have to" pattern. Conflation of "must" and "have to" syntax.
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - Sarah directly explained "must doesn't need 'to' after it"
- **Exercise priority:** 3

### #5 grammar/verb_tense (major) [02:04]

**Original:** Today I wake up on seven o'clock.
**Corrected:** Today I woke up at seven o'clock.

Used present simple "wake" instead of past simple "woke" for a completed past action. Also preposition error (see #6).

- **Communicative impact:** medium
- **L1 transfer:** partial - Ukrainian aspect system differs from English tense system. The present tense can be used for recent past events in casual Ukrainian speech.
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - Sarah explained "we say 'I woke up' because it was this morning - in the past"
- **Exercise priority:** 4
