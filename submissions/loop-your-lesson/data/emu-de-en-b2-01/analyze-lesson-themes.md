# Lesson themes: emu-de-en-b2-01

> Applied CEFR B2-C1 vocabulary descriptors to identify active vs. passive vocabulary and communicative functions. The lesson followed a clear pedagogical arc: content-driven discussion (climate change) used as a vehicle for pragmatic skill development (hedging, register awareness, polite disagreement). The teacher deliberately let the student's passion for the topic drive extended turns before layering in explicit language instruction. Themes were identified by tracking topic shifts, communicative function changes, and teacher-initiated pedagogical pivots.

## Lesson arc

The lesson progressed through three phases: (1) warm-up and rapport through personal updates (academic work, weekend plans, food culture), (2) extended content discussion about climate change using a documentary as springboard, and (3) explicit focus on pragmatic skills - hedging, register, and polite disagreement - with practice exercises applying these tools back to the climate change content. The teacher used Katrin's academic interests and strong opinions as natural material for language work, making the pragmatic instruction feel relevant rather than artificial. The final phase returned to cross-cultural comparison (German vs. British attitudes) as a consolidation activity.

### 1. Academic life and writing challenges [00:00-00:55]

**Function:** narrating, explaining | **Initiated by:** teacher (prompt), student (developed)

Katrin described her literature review on sustainable urban planning, discussing the challenge of adapting her German academic writing style to English conventions. She identified specific feedback from her supervisor about overly complex sentences, dense paragraphs, and excessive passive voice.

**Pedagogical intent:** Warm-up that reveals the student's academic context and metalinguistic awareness. Establishes the writing-style gap as a theme the teacher returns to later.
