# Lesson themes: emu-es-en-a1-01

> Applied CEFR A1 conversation descriptors to identify themes, vocabulary breadth, and communicative functions. The lesson followed a clear pedagogical arc from warm-up narration through structured vocabulary input (family, rooms) to guided production and review. Vocabulary was classified as active (student produced unprompted), passive (teacher-introduced, student repeated), or chunks (multi-word phrases the student encountered). Communicative functions mapped to CEFR A1 descriptors: describing people, describing places, expressing preferences, narrating simple past events.

## Lesson arc

The lesson progressed through four clear phases: (1) warm-up conversation about the weekend and life in London (00:00-04:20), building rapport and surfacing errors in a natural context; (2) family vocabulary and possessive adjectives (04:27-19:53), moving from known words to new vocabulary with structured practice; (3) describing people with adjectives (23:02-32:15), teaching adjective placement and building increasingly complex sentences; (4) home and room vocabulary with "there is/are" (32:38-45:01), applying the same describe-and-practice pattern to a new topic. The lesson ended with a review quiz (45:18-49:34) and homework assignment (50:01-51:33). The teacher consistently used explicit correction, self-correction encouragement, and L1 bridging (Spanish translations).

## Themes

### 1. Weekend and daily life in London [00:00-04:20]

**Function:** narrating, describing | **Initiated by:** teacher

Warm-up conversation about Carmen's weekend trip to Camden Market and her life as a university student in London. Surfaced key errors (past tense, null subjects, false friend "actually", preposition "to/in") in natural conversational flow.

**Pedagogical intent:** Activate prior knowledge, build rapport, diagnose error patterns in unstructured speech before the structured lesson.

#### Active vocabulary
| Term | Context | Level |
|------|---------|-------|
| weekend | "My weekend was very nice" | A1 |
| scarf | "a scarf for my mother" | A2 |
| cold | "Is very cold today in London" | A1 |
| university | "I like the university" | A1 |
| friends | "I have many friends from different countries" | A1 |

#### Passive vocabulary
| Term | Context | Level |
|------|---------|-------|
| currently | teacher introduced as correction for "actually" | A2 |
| getting used to | teacher introduced, Carmen asked for meaning | B1 |

#### Chunks
- "getting used to" (introduced by teacher, attempted by student)
- "how to say...?" (communication strategy, used repeatedly)
- "es que..." (L1 filler, used as discourse marker)

#### Representative exchange
> **Miguel:** So you went to Camden Market - remember, for the past, we say 'I went', not 'I go'.
> **Carmen:** Ah yes, I went to Camden Market. Is very nice there!
> **Miguel:** It is very nice there, yes! Remember we need 'it' at the beginning.

### 2. Family vocabulary and possessives [04:27-19:53]

**Function:** describing, identifying, listing | **Initiated by:** teacher (Carmen expressed enthusiasm)

Core vocabulary building phase. Teacher introduced family member terms, possessive adjectives (my, your, his, her, our, their), and age expression ("she is X years old"). Carmen practiced through describing her own family members - parents, siblings, grandparents, aunt, uncle, cousins, and pet.

**Pedagogical intent:** Build foundational family vocabulary, introduce and drill possessive adjectives, correct the "tener anos" age construction, practice 3rd person -s in context.

#### Passive vocabulary
| Term | Context | Level |

#### Chunks
- "X years old" (age expression, practiced repeatedly)
- "his name is / her name is" (possessive + identification)

#### Representative exchange
> **Carmen:** My sister Lucia, she have sixteen years.
> **Miguel:** In English we say 'she IS sixteen years old'. We use 'be', not 'have' for age.
> **Carmen:** She is sixteen years old! That is so strange.

### 3. Describing people with adjectives [23:02-32:15]

**Function:** describing, comparing | **Initiated by:** teacher

Teacher introduced personality and appearance adjectives, explicitly taught adjective-before-noun word order (contrasting with Spanish), and began comparatives. Carmen practiced describing each family member using two-three adjective combinations.

**Pedagogical intent:** Expand descriptive vocabulary, drill pre-nominal adjective placement (L1 interference point), introduce comparative forms as preview.

### 4. Home, rooms, and furniture [32:38-45:01]

**Function:** describing places, comparing, expressing existence | **Initiated by:** teacher

Vocabulary building for rooms (kitchen, living room, bedroom, bathroom, dining room, garage, garden) and furniture (bed, desk, wardrobe, sofa, coffee table, bookshelf, sink, mirror). Practiced "there is/are" for describing room contents. Carmen compared her family home in Valencia with her student flat in London.

**Pedagogical intent:** Expand place vocabulary, drill "there is/are" with singular/plural agreement, practice prepositions of place (on/in), apply adjective word order in new context.
