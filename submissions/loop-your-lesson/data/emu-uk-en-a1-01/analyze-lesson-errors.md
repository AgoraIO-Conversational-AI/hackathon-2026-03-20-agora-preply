# Error analysis: emu-uk-en-a1-01

> Applied the error taxonomy (grammar, vocabulary, pragmatic categories) with severity calibrated against CEFR A1 descriptors. Ukrainian-English interlanguage patterns from theory/interlanguage were used to distinguish L1 transfer errors (age expression with "have", preposition "on" for "to", zero-article system) from developmental errors common to all A1 learners (plural -s omission, 3rd person -s omission). At A1, verb tense errors in narration and article omission are expected and rated moderate rather than major; however, systematic SVA omission and L1 preposition transfer are flagged as high-priority exercise targets because they impede communication clarity.

## Errors

### #1 grammar/verb_tense (moderate) [00:28]

**Original:** "I go to park"
**Corrected:** "I went to the park"

Used present simple "go" to narrate a past event (weekend). Also missing definite article "the" before "park".

- **Communicative impact:** medium - listener must infer time reference from context
- **L1 transfer:** Ukrainian uses context and aspect rather than tense morphology to indicate past; zero-article language
- **Correction strategy:** recast - Sarah responded "You went to the park"
- **Exercise priority:** 4

### #4 grammar/verb_tense + vocabulary/calque (major) [01:20]

**Original:** "She have seven years. And son have four."
**Corrected:** "She is seven years old. And my son is four."

Two errors: (1) L1 transfer of "maty roky" (to have years) instead of English "to be X years old"; (2) missing 3rd person -s on "have" (though entire construction is wrong).

- **Communicative impact:** high - native speaker may not understand age expression
- **L1 transfer:** direct calque from Ukrainian "ij sim rokiv" (she has seven years); Slavic languages express age with "have"
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - Sarah explained "In English we say 'she is seven years old', not 'she has seven years'"
- **Exercise priority:** 5

- **Correction strategy:** recast - Sarah said "The children played and you sat and watched"
- **Exercise priority:** 4

### #6 grammar/preposition + verb_tense (major) [02:25]

**Original:** "I go on shop"
**Corrected:** "I went to the shop"

Two errors: (1) preposition "on" instead of "to" (L1 transfer from Ukrainian "na" which covers both "on" and "to"); (2) present tense for past.

- **Communicative impact:** high - "on shop" is confusing to native speakers
- **L1 transfer:** Ukrainian preposition "na" maps to both English "on" and "to"; "ity na mahazyn" = "go to shop"
- **Correction strategy:** explicit + elicitation - Sarah explained and prompted "I went to...?"
- **Exercise priority:** 5
