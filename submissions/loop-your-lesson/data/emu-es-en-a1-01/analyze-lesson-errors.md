# Error analysis: emu-es-en-a1-01

> Applied the error taxonomy (grammar, vocabulary, pragmatic categories) with severity calibrated against CEFR A1 descriptors. L1 transfer analysis used Spanish-English interlanguage patterns (null subjects, ser/estar-have/be age construction, false friends, adjective placement, preposition mapping). ## Errors

### #1 grammar/verb_tense (major) [00:20]

**Original:** "I go to Camden Market"
**Corrected:** "I went to Camden Market"

Student used present simple instead of past simple when narrating a weekend event. Context clearly required past tense ("how was your weekend").

- **Communicative impact:** medium - listener can infer past from context but form is wrong
- **L1 transfer:** partial - Spanish also requires past tense here ("fui"), so this is a developmental error (incomplete acquisition of irregular past forms) rather than direct transfer
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - teacher directly stated the rule ("for the past, we say 'I went'")
- **Exercise priority:** 4

### #2 grammar/null_subject (moderate) [00:38]

**Original:** "Is very nice there!"
**Corrected:** "It is very nice there!"

Missing dummy subject "it". Spanish allows null subjects ("Es muy bonito alli") but English requires an explicit subject.

- **Communicative impact:** low - meaning is clear
- **L1 transfer:** yes - Spanish is a pro-drop language; "es" does not require an overt subject. This is direct syntactic transfer from L1.
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - teacher modeled the correct form and stated the rule
- **Exercise priority:** 5

### #3 grammar/null_subject (moderate) [00:56]

**Original:** "Is very cold today in London!"

- **Communicative impact:** low
- **L1 transfer:** yes - pro-drop transfer, reinforced by Spanish weather constructions that use impersonal verbs
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - teacher restated rule about English needing a subject
- **Exercise priority:** 5

### #5 vocabulary/false_friend (moderate) [03:13]

**Original:** "Actually I live in London for three months now"
**Corrected:** "Currently I live in London for three months now"

Used "actually" (en realidad) when meaning "actualmente" (currently). Classic Spanish-English false friend.

- **Communicative impact:** medium - changes the meaning of the sentence
- **L1 transfer:** yes - direct lexical transfer; "actualmente" looks like "actually" but means "currently"
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - teacher explained the difference and provided the correct word
- **Exercise priority:** 4

### #8 grammar/verb_form (major) [09:50]

**Original:** "She have sixteen years"
**Corrected:** "She is sixteen years old"

Double error: (a) used "have" instead of "be" for age, and (b) missing 3rd person -s on "have". Spanish expresses age with "tener" (to have): "tiene dieciseis anos."

- **Communicative impact:** high - could confuse a listener unfamiliar with this L1 pattern
- **L1 transfer:** yes - direct structural transfer from "tener + anos" construction
- **Correction strategy:** explicit - teacher explained the Spanish/English difference in expressing age
- **Exercise priority:** 5
