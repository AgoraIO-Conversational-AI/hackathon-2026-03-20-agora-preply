# Lesson themes: exp-uttsplit-15

> Themes were identified by tracking topic transitions, speaker initiative, and content clusters across the 64-minute transcript. Communicative functions were assigned based on the dominant language use in each segment (explaining for the hackathon discussion, analyzing for the error exercise, describing for the clothing theme, narrating for family stories, negotiating for scheduling). Vocabulary was classified as active (student-produced in spontaneous speech) vs. passive (teacher-introduced or encountered in reading texts) with CEFR levels estimated against the companion volume descriptors for vocabulary range. The pedagogical arc was inferred from the teacher's sequencing choices: warm-up -> extended narration -> controlled practice -> text + discussion -> explicit instruction -> wrap-up, which follows a principled communicative methodology. The clothing theme was the lesson's main pedagogical target, supported by the error exercise as a grammar interlude. Student-initiated themes (hackathon, family) were skillfully leveraged by the teacher as authentic contexts for language practice.

## Lesson arc

The 64-minute lesson followed a classic communicative teaching arc: free conversation warm-up (family/health), then extended student-led narration about his hackathon work (maximizing authentic talk time in Polish), transitioning to a structured error-correction exercise drilling verb+preposition collocations (a known Ukrainian-Polish interference area), then a fashion/clothing theme combining authentic text reading with personalized discussion and explicit vocabulary instruction (wkładać/zakładać/ubierać distinction). Throughout, the teacher wove personal topics (children, shopping habits, style memories) into language learning goals, maintaining high student motivation. The pedagogical progression moved from fluency-focused free production to accuracy-focused controlled practice and back to production, with vocabulary input systematically embedded via reading texts and teacher-guided discussion.
