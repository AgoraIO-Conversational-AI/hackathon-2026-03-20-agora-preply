**Overall level: A1+** | Confidence: high

## Zone of Proximal Development

- **Lower bound:** A1 - can independently produce simple present sentences about familiar topics (family, home), use basic possessives (my, his, her), describe people with pre-taught adjectives, use "there is" for existence.
- **Upper bound:** A2 - approaching ability to use 3rd person -s consistently, form basic questions with inversion, use "there is/are" with correct number agreement, distinguish on/in/at prepositions, avoid null subjects.
- **Stretch topics:** past simple (irregular verbs), comparatives/superlatives, present continuous vs simple present, "have to / need to" for obligation, prepositions of time (at/on/in).

## Gaps

### 3rd person singular -s (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
8 omission errors across the lesson (work, run, don't/doesn't, have/has, live, help, like, live). This is the most frequent and systematic error. While she self-corrects some instances, the base rate of omission is high. This is the primary blocker for A2 reclassification.

### Null subject / dummy "it" (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
5 instances of omitting "it" in subject position ("Is very cold", "Is because", "Is the national sport"). Direct L1 transfer from Spanish pro-drop. Needs explicit drilling to build automaticity.

### Preposition selection (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
Errors with in/to (direction vs location), in/on (surfaces), in/at (holidays/time). Spanish "en" maps to multiple English prepositions. Requires context-specific practice.

### False friends (Vocabulary) [exercise_focus: true]
"Actually/currently" and "sympathetic/friendly" errors. High communicative impact because they change meaning. Carmen is aware of the issue but needs reinforcement.

### Adjective word order (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: false]
Multiple instances of post-nominal placement ("a woman very nice", "a television very big"), but Carmen consistently self-corrects by end of lesson. This gap is closing; lower exercise priority.

### Age expression (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: false]
"She have sixteen years" instead of "she is sixteen years old." Addressed and corrected during lesson; Carmen applied the correct form consistently after instruction. Monitor in next lesson.

### Question formation (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: false]
"Why you don't have...?" and "How old she is?" show incomplete inversion in questions. Only 1-2 instances; insufficient data for strong conclusion. Monitor.

## Suggestions

### For teacher
1. **[P1]** Dedicate a focused drill segment on 3rd person -s in next lesson. Use high-frequency verbs (works, lives, plays, has, goes, does). Consider a categorizer activity: "Which form is correct - he work / he works?"
2. **[P2]** Create a recurring warm-up exercise where Carmen describes someone's daily routine (forces repeated 3rd person -s usage in context).
3. **[P2]** Introduce a "null subject spotter" game - read sentences with and without "it" and have Carmen identify which are correct.
4. **[P3]** Continue L1 bridging (Spanish translations) - Carmen responds very well to contrastive explanations. Explicitly compare Spanish and English preposition systems.
5. **[P3]** Leverage Carmen's Netflix habit - suggest she note 3rd person -s usage while watching.

### For student
1. **[P1]** Practice writing 5 sentences per day about someone else using "he/she" - focus on adding -s to every verb (he works, she lives, it rains).
2. **[P1]** When speaking, pause before every "he/she/it" verb and consciously add the -s. This will feel slow at first but builds automaticity.
3. **[P2]** Make a list of common false friends: actually/currently, sympathetic/friendly, excited/excitada. Write one sentence for each to reinforce correct usage.
4. **[P2]** Practice "it is..." sentences: write 5 weather sentences (it is cold, it is sunny) and 5 opinion sentences (it is difficult, it is nice) each day.
5. **[P3]** For prepositions, learn fixed phrases rather than rules: "go TO school", "live IN London", "at Christmas", "on the phone", "on the floor."
