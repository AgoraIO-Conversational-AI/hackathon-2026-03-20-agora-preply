# Lesson themes: emu-uk-en-a1-02

> Applied CEFR A1-A2 vocabulary and communicative function descriptors to identify themes, vocabulary tiers, and the pedagogical arc. The lesson follows a classic communicative approach: warm-up with personalized context, presentation of new language (telling time), guided practice with daily routine vocabulary, grammar focus on present simple and frequency adverbs, and free production through extended descriptions of routines. The teacher consistently uses the student's real life as context, which maximizes engagement and memorability at A1.

## Lesson arc

The lesson follows a clear four-stage pedagogical progression: (1) warm-up and needs analysis through casual conversation about work, (2) presentation of telling-the-time vocabulary (half past, quarter past/to, past/to), (3) guided practice combining time expressions with daily routine verbs in present simple, and (4) extended production tasks requiring the student to describe routines for himself, his wife, and his friend using third person -s and frequency adverbs. The teacher scaffolds complexity incrementally - first person routines, then third person, then negatives, then adverbs of frequency - culminating in a multi-paragraph monologue about a typical Monday. Homework extends the cycle with a writing task (Saturday paragraph) and a real-world practice task (saying clock times mentally).
