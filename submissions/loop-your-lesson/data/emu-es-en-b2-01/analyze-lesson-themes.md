# Lesson themes: emu-es-en-b2-01

> Applied CEFR B2 conversation descriptors to identify themes, vocabulary patterns, and communicative functions. The lesson followed a clear pedagogical arc from warm-up personal narrative through structured debate to practical skill application (email writing), finishing with error consolidation. Vocabulary was assessed against B2-C1 boundary expectations. Themes were cross-referenced with error patterns to identify which topics triggered the most L1 transfer.

## Lesson arc

The lesson progresses through four distinct phases: (1) warm-up and personal narrative about work [00:00-03:00], (2) structured discussion and debate on remote/hybrid work and AI [03:00-31:00], (3) practical application via formal email writing [34:27-42:56], (4) error review, consolidation, and homework [42:56-59:17]. The teacher skillfully connected Pablo's real-world consulting experience to language learning goals, using his genuine need to write a schedule-change email as a vehicle for register and formality practice. The debate sections served dual purposes: building argumentative fluency and surfacing fossilized errors under communicative pressure.

### 5. Error consolidation and collocations drill [18:30-24:10]

**Function:** metalinguistic analysis, drilling | **Initiated by:** teacher (error review segment)

Teacher shifted to explicit error focus, reviewing collocations (make/do/give/take/hold/attend), false friends (actually/currently, assist/attend, sensible/sensitive, comprehensive/understanding), and discourse markers (moreover, on the other hand). Included an interactive quiz where teacher tested Pablo on correct collocations.

**Pedagogical intent:** Consolidate error corrections from the conversation phase; build explicit knowledge of collocations and false friends.

| Term | Context | Level |
|------|---------|-------|

- "make a decision" (not take)
