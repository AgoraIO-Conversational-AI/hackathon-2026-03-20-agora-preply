## Gaps

### Past tense marking (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
Systematic use of present simple for past narration. Six instances across the lesson. Only uses past forms when explicitly prompted or in memorized chunks. This is the primary grammatical gap blocking A2.

### Article system (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
Zero-article transfer from Ukrainian. Missing both definite ("the weather", "the park") and indefinite ("an apple", "a tomato") articles. Three clear instances plus numerous minor omissions. Expected at A1 for Ukrainian speakers but needs focused practice.

### 3rd person -s (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
Systematic omission of present simple 3rd person -s: "she have", "my family like", "bread cost", "she want". Four clear instances. L1 transfer - Ukrainian has no equivalent marking.

### Plural marking (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: true]
Omits plural -s after numbers and quantifiers: "two pound", "four tomato", "many dish", "three apple". Four instances. Partially self-corrected by end of lesson.

### Preposition selection (Accuracy) [exercise_focus: false]
L1 transfer of Ukrainian "na" as "on" instead of "to": "I go on shop". Single major instance. Lower priority as it was explicitly corrected and student showed immediate uptake.

### Adverb/adjective distinction (Range) [exercise_focus: false]
Used "good" instead of "well" as adverb: "I don't speak English good". Single instance. L1 transfer from Ukrainian "dobre" which covers both.

## Suggestions

### For teacher
1. **[P1]** Focus next 2-3 lessons on past simple (regular -ed and high-frequency irregulars: went, bought, cooked, had). Use weekend narration as recurring warm-up to drill naturally.
2. **[P1]** Introduce article practice through the food/shopping context already established. "The bread" (specific), "a tomato" (one of many), "some milk" (uncountable) as a triad.
3. **[P2]** Build 3rd person -s awareness through describing family routines: "She likes...", "He wants...", "It costs...". Connect to shopping context ("The milk costs...").
4. **[P2]** Continue role-play approach - student showed dramatic improvement from first to third attempt. Increase complexity gradually (asking for directions next, as student requested).
5. **[P3]** Assign listening homework - student identified fast speech as main real-world barrier [03:50].

### For student
1. **[P1]** When talking about yesterday or last week, always use past forms: "I went", "I bought", "I cooked" (not "I go", "I buy", "I cook"). Practice saying 5 past sentences each evening.
2. **[P1]** Before a noun, think: can I count it? Yes = use "a" or "an". No = use "some". Practice: "an apple", "a tomato", "some milk", "some bread".
3. **[P2]** For he/she/it, add -s to the verb: "she likes", "he wants", "it costs". Practice with family descriptions: "My daughter likes...", "My son wants..."
4. **[P2]** After numbers, always add -s: "two pounds", "three apples", "four tomatoes".
5. **[P3]** Visit a shop this week and use: "Can I have... please?", "How much is it?", "Sorry, can you speak slowly please?"
